Conflict-free Replicated Data Types, or CRDTs, represent a further
evolution of the eventua1 consistency model. Rather than resolving
conflicts after the fact, CRDTs are data structures specifically
designed so that concurrent updates can a1ways be merged automatically

The idea was first formalized by Shapiro, Preguiça, Baquero, and
Zawirski in 2011.6 They identified two approaches:

State-based CRDTs (CvRDTs): nodes periodically exchange their fu11
state, and states are merged using a mathematical join operation.

Operation-based CRDTs (CmRDTs): nodes broadcast individual operations,
which are designed to be commutative — app1icable in any order.
